Themes of research and main results

Security of end-in-end for co-operative systems ITS
We continued the works undertaken in the Cifre thesis with Renault entitled Securing Future Cooperative ITS Applications within the framework as of projects ISE and SCOOP@F.
The details are given in the sections (Activities at the VeDeCoM Institute/Activities with the IRT SystemX)
In the thesis in Co-supervision (ParisTech Telecom – NTU (Singapore)) of Heng Chuan Tan who relates to the mechanisms of security in the vehicular networks with a grid, we suggested a management solution of keys lightweight which consitute an alternative to the approach which uses the certificates with an architecture IGC (infrastructure of management of keys or PKI (Public Key Infrstructure)).
Our objective is to reduce the cost of calculation in the embarked units (in the vehicles and the units edge of road), to reduce the indication of the communication protocols while ensuring the security level required in an environment on a large scale.
Several tenders are in hand (2 reviews and 1 conference).

Co-operation networks STOWNET – cellular networks 4G

We continued work of the year 2013-2014.
Publications are in the course of tender on a proposal which optimizes the unloading of cells 4G in the case of a transmission multi-flow.
We proposed an analytical model, named FOSAA, which evaluates the capacity of a vehicular network to support a cellular traffic in a context of offload of the cellular infrastructure by the means of communications V2V (vehicle with vehicle) and I2V (vehicle with infrastructure) opportunist.
FOSAA takes into account the constraints relating to the existence of links V2V, I2V; capacity of the channel; aggregation; the time of activity of the nodes and the saturation of the channel.
Simulations showed the effectiveness of FOSAA to select the traffic adequate multi-flow with offloader.

Modeling of the co-operation by means of the theory of games

We studied the use of approach EGT for adjuster the number of nodes relay runners in order to disseminate information in a network ad hoc vehicular by optimizing the rate of dissemination and the use of the band-width.
Based on our previous works published in the conference IEEE ICC2014, we spread the work.
Two publications were subjected (an article subjected to IEEE VTC' 16 and one article sousmis to the newspaper IEEE Transactions one Vehicular Technology (the second resubmission following corrections).
